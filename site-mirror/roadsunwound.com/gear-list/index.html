New Gear List:

Bike

The Black Cat Hostel, xmas home in Xela

View at Picasa

 

I have switched out some of the gear for the route south from Cancun. Primarily it is the change to a Surly Troll from the vaunted Long Haul Trucker.

Surly Troll frame and fork
-I am finding the bike to be a perfectly versatile machine for any road. If I had the financial ability I would run a front shock to aid in dirt road abuse.
Avid bb7 mechanical disc brakes (front and rear) – very loud, but good at stopping.
Avid brake levers
Alex Adventure Rims (26″ x 36 hole, disc)
Schwalbe Marathon Tires (26″ x 2.25″)
-Schwalbe has retired the Marathon XR’s and replaced them with the Mondial.
Shimano XT hubs (front and rear)
Shimano XT derailleurs (front and rear)
Shimano XT mountain crankset (22x32x44)
Shimano XT 9-speed cassette (11-32)
Sram 9-speed chain
Stylo Handlebars (10 degree sweep, flat bar)
Ergon GC2 grips
Specialized Pro-Set adjustable stem
Old Man Mountain Cold Springs front rack
Surly rear rack
Cane Creek Thudbuster ST seatpost
Brooks saddle (original)
Shimano pedals (original)
Chris King Headset (original)
Cateye Adventure bike computer

Equipment:
Montbell Thunderdome 2 person tent – replaced by a North Face Rock 22 in Guatemala.
– In a frustrating turn of events, my rainfly was stolen off the beach in Guatemala. I have replaced the tent with a North Face Rock 22 because it was available locally and wouldn’t involve complex international shipping. I have yet to use it, but at first glance it seems large, heavy and a bit cumbersome. The Montbell tent had a unique four way connecter at the apex of the poles making set up lightning fast – a good thing in rainy weather. Not convinced of the North Face design, but will relay info once I use it.
Montbell UL Super Spiral Down Hugger 3 (30 degree sleeping bag)
Montbell Down Sheet (warm weather use and as a liner for cold nights)

Original Bike – Alaska to LA and Great Divide:
Surly Long Haul Trucker –
A workhorse of a bike that is the go to option for many people new to touring. I find it suited much more to paved roads under any load, but see limitations with its off-road abilities (admittedly, it isn’t designed for intense off-road touring). I have switched (fall ’11) to a new Surly Troll to expand my exploring abilities and reduce my tire size to the worldwide 26″ standard.
